PROBLEM

I was getting frustrated with a lot of Bioware RPGs and wanted to make a game that tackled my core problems with them:

  • Cognitive dissonance between action and choice
  • Unreasonable time investment required
PROCESS

Research and development was my main focus. The project necessitated unique game design and quick development. A lot of self-learning and an old C#/XNA game engine laid the framework I needed.

I couldn't do it all on my own though. I worked with a chiptune musician I found on the internet and reached out to many journalists to get the word out. There was a lot of communication involved for the final product.

RESULTS

In 6 months I created and released a game that:

  • Determined your choices by your actions
  • Delivered a succinct story in a 15 minute playthrough
  • Utilized procedural terrain generation to drive engagement
  • Explored power themes through unique combat mechanics

While not a commercial success, there were hundreds of downloads and half the proceeds went to Child's Play.

PROBLEM

TELUS' navigation needed improvement and the search had been stagnant for 4 years. I was allowed to take on this task in a 5 month timeframe.

PROCESS

I wrote a Java application from scratch, using REST services for fetching results. HTML5 and CSS3 were used to code an excellent front-end experience.

The TELUS visual brand, UX standards, and approval process were closely followed to gain stakeholder support. This became vital when championing Google site search and the organizational change required.

RESULTS

I was able to deliver a page which improved the navigation across telus.com and telusmobility.com. I was given personal recognition for my efforts by both the team's Director and VP.

In addition, this was a great personal learning experience as both a Project Manager and Developer.

PROBLEM

3D platforming games are taking a notable turn away from exploration. The focus is on linear challenges or puzzles which are fun but not what I want. I've made a couple attempts at making a game that rewarded exploration just as much as it rewarded progression.

PROCESS

Two attempts were made at the same game, first in C#/XNA and later in Unity3D/Javascript.

One of the major components was developing a free-floating camera that intelligently takes the best shot. There are a lot of cool little details like y-axis stabilization and momentum leading.

However, the core of it was developing "kinaesthetics". A lot of effort went into making the gameplay system feel great and giving a sense of occupying space to bring the world to life.

RESULTS

The first attempt was a proof of concept to myself with a self-imposed deadline. Development wasn't quick enough to warrant continuing until my second attempt in Unity3D. I had more reasonable goals the second time but a full-time job made it frustrating to make the progress I needed.

Despite not reaching full product, the engine development has been a very enlightening set of experiences. I look forward to getting the itch in the future and returning to it.
